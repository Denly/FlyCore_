CaCl2 (0.1M) Thaw a 1-ml 1M CaCl2 aliquot in 100 mL H2O. Sterilize by passage through a 0.45-µm filter. Chill on ice. 4°C
CaCl2 (1M) Dissolve 44g CaCl2-6H2O in 200 ml H2O. Sterilize by passage through a 0.2-µm filter. Store in 1-ml aliquots at -20°C. -20°C
EDTA (0.5 M pH 8.0, ethylenediamenetetraacetic acid) Add 186.1 g of disodium EDTA•2H2O to 800 mL of H2O. Stir vigorously on a magnetic stirrer. Adjust the pH to 8.0 with NaOH (~20 g of NaOH pellets). Adjust the volume to 1 L with H2O. Dispense into aliquots and sterilize by autoclaving. The disodium salt of EDTA will not go into solution until the pH of the solution is adjusted to ~8.0 by the addition of NaOH. Room Temp. November 29, 2010
Glucose (1M) Dissolving 18 g of glucose in 90 mL of H2O. After the sugar has dissolved, adjust the volume of the solution to 100 mL with H2O and sterilize by passing it through a 0.22-µm filter.
LB (Luria-Bertani) medium Add 10 g Bacto®-tryptone, 5 g Bacto®-yeast extract and 5 g NaCl to deionized water to a final volume of 1 liter. Adjust the pH to 7.5 with 10N NaOH, and autoclave to sterilize. Store at room temperature in 100 ml aliquots. 4°C December 9, 2010
MgCl2 (1M) To prepare 1 L of 1 M MgCl2, dissolve 203.3 g of MgCl2•6H2O in 800 mL H2O. Adjust the volume to 1 L using H2O. Dispense into aliquots and sterilize by autoclaving. Room Temp. December 2, 2010
MgSO4 (1M, Magnesium sulfate) To prepare a 1 M solution: Dissolve 12 g of MgSO4 in a final volume of 100 mL of H2O. Sterilize by autoclaving or filter sterilization. Store at room temperature. Room Temp.
NaCl (5M) Mix 146.1 g NaCl in ~350 ml H2O. Dissolve, then bring up volume to 500ml with H2O. Sterilize by autoclaving for 20 minutes. Room Temp. November 29, 2010
NaOAc (3M, pH 5.2, Sodium acetate) Dissolve 408.3 g of sodium acetate•3H2O in 800 mL of H2O. Adjust the pH to 5.2 with glacial acetic acid. Adjust the volume to 1 L with H2O. Dispense into aliquots and sterilize by autoclaving. Room Temp.
NaOH (10N, Sodium hydroxide) The preparation of 10 N NaOH involves a highly exothermic reaction, which can cause breakage of glass containers. Prepare this solution with extreme care in plastic beakers. To 800 mL of H2O, slowly add 400 g of NaOH pellets, stirring continuously. As an added precaution, place the beaker on ice. When the pellets have dissolved completely, adjust the volume to 1 L with H2O. Store the solution in a plastic container at room temperature. Sterilization is not necessary. Room Temp December 2, 2010
Potassium Acetate (3M) Mix 60ml of 5 M potassium acetate and 5 M potassium acetate, 11.5 ml glacial acetic acid and 28.5ml. The resulting solution is 3 M with respect to potassium and 5 M with respect to acetate. Store the solution at 4°C and transfer it to an ice bucket just before use. 4°C
Potassium acetate (5M) Add 49.07 g Potassium acetate into ~70 ml H2O. Dissolve, then bring up to 100ml with H2O. Sterilize by autoclaving for 20 minutes. Room Temp.
SDS (10%, sodium dodecyl sulfate or sodium lauryl sulfate) Dissolve 10 g of SDS in 80 mL of H2O, and then add H2O to 100 mL. This stock solution is stable for 6 mo at room temperature. Room Temp.
SOC medium To 80 ml of deionized H20, add 2 g of tryptone, 0.5 g of yeast extract, and 0.05 g of NaCl. Mix until the solutes have dissolved. Add 0.5 ml of 0.5 M KCl solution. Adjust the pH to 7.0 with 10 N NaOH (~ 0.01 ml). Adjust the volume to 100 ml with deionized H20. Autoclave for 20 minutes. Cool to 60°C or less. Add 1 ml of sterile 1 M glucose solution, 1 ml of sterile 1 M MgCl2, and 1 ml of 1 M MgSO4. Sterilize the solution by filtration through a 0.22-micron filter. Room Temp.
SSC (20X) Dissolve 175.3g NaCl and 88.2g sodium citrate in 800ml of water. Adjust pH to 7.0 with 10N NaOH. Adjust volume to 1 liter. Dispense into aliquots. Sterilize by autoclaving.
TAE (50X stock) Add 242 g of Tris base, 57.1 mL of acetic acid (glacial) and 100 mL of 0.5 M EDTA (pH 8.0) into 1L stock. The 1X working solution is 40 mM Tris-acetate/1 mM EDTA. Room Temp. November 29, 2010
TE (10 mM Tris plus 1mM EDTA, pH 8.0) To make a 100 ml solution of TE Buffer, 1 ml of 1 M Tris HCl (pH 8.0) and 0.2 ml EDTA (0.5 M) and make up with double distilled water up to 100ml. Room Temp.
Tris (1M, pH 7.5) Dissolve 121.1 g of Tris base in 800 mL of H2O. Adjust the pH to the desired value by adding concentrated HCl (~65ml). Room Temp. November 29, 2010
Tris (1M, pH 8.0) Dissolve 121.1 g of Tris base in 800 mL of H2O. Adjust the pH to the desired value by adding concentrated HCl (~42ml). Room Temp. December 2, 2010
